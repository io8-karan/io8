Our Divine Destinations

Kedarnath

Perched amidst the majestic Garhwal Himalayas, Kedarnath is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. The spiritual energy, combined with the stunning natural landscape, makes it an unforgettable pilgrimage. Accessible mostly by foot or pony, it's a testament to devotion.

View Details

Badrinath

Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, Badrinath is a vital part of the Char Dham Yatra. Its vibrant architecture stands against a backdrop of snow-clad peaks and the Alaknanda River, offering a serene and profoundly spiritual experience. The temple's ancient history draws devotees from across the globe.

View Details

Vaishno Devi

Nestled in the Trikuta Mountains, the holy cave shrine of Vaishno Devi is one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in India. Millions of pilgrims undertake the arduous trek to seek blessings from Mata Rani, experiencing immense faith and devotion along the way.

View Details

Naina Devi

The revered Naina Devi Temple, a significant Shakti Peeth, is situated atop a hill in Bilaspur, Himachal Pradesh. It offers panoramic views of the Govind Sagar Lake and surrounding hills, combining spiritual reverence with natural splendor.

View Details

Mansa Devi

Located atop the Bilwa Parvat in Haridwar, Mansa Devi Temple is another prominent Shakti Peeth. Accessible by cable car or a trek, it provides breathtaking views of the city and the Ganga River, a truly divine experience for devotees.

View Details

Ram Mandir, Ayodhya

The newly constructed Ram Mandir in Ayodhya marks the birthplace of Lord Rama. This grand temple is a symbol of faith and devotion for millions, attracting pilgrims to witness its architectural marvel and spiritual significance in the sacred city.
